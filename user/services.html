About Vizhinjam Seaport

A Gateway to Global Trade

Vizhinjam International Seaport is a deep-water multipurpose port being developed near Thiruvananthapuram, Kerala. Located just 10 nautical miles from the international shipping route and close to the East–West global maritime trade corridor, it has a natural depth of more than 20 meters, making it ideal for accommodating large container vessels and mega ships without extensive dredging.

The project is designed as a Public–Private Partnership (PPP) between the Government of Kerala and Adani Ports. Once fully operational, Vizhinjam will serve as a transshipment hub, significantly reducing India’s dependence on foreign ports such as Colombo, Dubai, and Singapore for cargo handling.

Key Features
  • ✔ Natural deep-sea harbor requiring minimal dredging
  • ✔ State-of-the-art container handling facilities
  • ✔ Multimodal connectivity through road, rail, and air links
  • ✔ Ability to handle mega ships and mother vessels
  • ✔ Scope for cruise tourism and maritime services

Beyond trade, the port is expected to bring economic growth, job creation, and infrastructural development in Kerala, positioning it as a major player in the global shipping industry.
